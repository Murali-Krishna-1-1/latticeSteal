Connection Detailing

A disciplined, data-driven approach to steel connection detailing that ensures structural integrity, constructability, and AISC compliance in Tekla Structures and SDS/2.

Our Approach to Connection Detailing

At Lattice Steel, connection detailing is treated as a controlled technical process, not a template-driven task. Each connection is developed only after validating design inputs, calculation data, and fabrication requirements to ensure accuracy and constructability.

Three Key Data Inputs

Engineer of Record (EOR)

  • • Reaction loads (shear, moment, axial)
  • • Governing design codes and criteria
  • • Standard connection details
  • • Material grade requirements

Connection Engineer

  • • Design calculation packages
  • • Plate sizes, thicknesses, and geometry
  • • Weld sizes and bolt strengths
  • • Stiffeners, doublers, and continuity plates

Fabricator Preferences

  • • Preferred bolt sizes and stock
  • • Shop equipment and CNC constraints
  • • Shipping and handling limitations
  • • Field erection clearances

Application in the 3D Model

Load Verification

Each modeled connection is verified against EOR reactions or connection engineer calculations to ensure correct load transfer.

Clash & Clearance Checks

Connections are reviewed for clashes with adjacent members, MEP systems, and architectural finishes during early modeling stages.

Technical RFIs

When inconsistencies are identified, clear RFIs are prepared with annotated model views before fabrication begins.

Data Integration Summary
